Foundation

What is Engineering Human Performance?

Understanding the systematic methodology that drives real workplace transformation.

🔬
Engineering Human Performance (EHP) is a systematic approach employed to analyze performance, identify performance issues' root causes, and design evidence-based solutions to enhance human performance in the workplace. It's not just about training — it's about engineering the right conditions for people to thrive.
😓

From Incompetence

Gaps, frustration, missed targets

→
🚀

To Competence

Clarity, capability, peak results

Theory & Model

The HPT Model

Van Tiem, Moseley & Dessinger (2012) — the gold standard framework for Human Performance Technology.

Analysis
  • GAP analysis — current vs desired state
  • Personal causes: skill, knowledge, motivation
  • Environmental causes: tools, processes, incentives
Intervention Selection
  • Instructional approach (training, e-learning)
  • Non-instructional approach (job aids, process change)
  • Matching solution to root cause
Implementation
  • Partnering & consultation with stakeholders
  • Project management and rollout
  • Communication and change management
📋

Evaluation — Connected to All Phases

Evaluation runs throughout the entire process — not just at the end. It continuously informs decisions at every stage of the HPT model.

Gilbert's Framework

Human Performance Intervention Concepts

Thomas F. Gilbert's four instructional interventions for learning and development — summarized with real intervention examples.

📋
Information
Providing clear information about job tasks and expectations so employees know what's expected of them.
Intervention Example
Guidelines or documentation with detailed steps and work procedures
⚡
Empowerment
Granting employees responsibility and autonomy in their work to boost ownership and quality of decisions.
Intervention Example
Training and support to help employees make confident, independent decisions
🏗️
Job Design
Organizing job tasks to align with the abilities and skills of employees for optimal fit and performance.
Intervention Example
Modifying task structures or responsibilities to match individual skills and strengths
🎓
Education & Training
Providing the knowledge and skills needed to perform tasks effectively and grow professionally.
Intervention Example
Training programs tailored to the specific needs of individuals or groups
Real-World Application

Case Studies

See how Engineering Human Performance is applied across different industries — and how cause analysis changes the entire solution strategy.

📋 Situation

  • A regional hospital noticed medication error rates were 3× above the national benchmark
  • Staff blamed overwork; management blamed lack of training
  • Previous training-only approach had shown no measurable improvement

🔍 EHP Analysis Found

  • Root cause: unclear labeling + shift handoff communication gaps — NOT a skill deficit
  • Only 20% of errors were skill-related; 80% were environmental/process issues
  • Nurses had knowledge but lacked clear information and standardized procedures

⚙️ Interventions Designed

  • Information: Redesigned medication labels + digital checklist at point-of-care
  • Job Design: Restructured shift handoff protocol with mandatory verbal + written confirmation
  • Training: 2-hour focused module only for the 20% skill-based gap

📈 Results After 6 Months

Reduction in medication errors

💡

Key Lesson: EHP revealed that training was NOT the solution — process redesign and information clarity were. Without proper cause analysis, the hospital would have wasted resources on ineffective training.

📋 Situation

  • Electronics factory saw a 22% increase in product defects over two quarters
  • Production manager wanted a week-long technical retraining for all 200+ workers
  • Budget was approved, but similar past training had produced poor results

🔍 EHP Analysis Found

  • New machinery introduced 3 months ago had inconsistent calibration settings
  • Workers were never shown updated quality tolerances for new component specs
  • Incentive system was rewarding speed over accuracy

⚙️ Interventions Designed

  • Environmental: Fixed calibration + daily machine audit checklist
  • Information: Visual quality spec cards posted at each workstation
  • Empowerment: Workers given authority to stop the line for suspected defects
  • Incentives: Restructured bonus tied to quality score, not pure output speed

📈 Results After 3 Months

Reduction in defect rate
15%

$0

💡

Key Lesson: A proposed $80,000 retraining program was replaced by low-cost environmental and process interventions. EHP's cause analysis saved the company both money and time — with dramatically better outcomes.

📋 Situation

  • A 50-person SaaS startup had high developer turnover (40% annual churn)
  • Exit interviews cited "unclear role expectations" and "no growth path"
  • HR proposed a compensation increase; leadership wanted a culture overhaul

🔍 EHP Analysis Found

  • No formal onboarding — new hires took 4+ months to reach productivity
  • Role scopes were ambiguous; developers frequently did tasks outside their expertise
  • Zero feedback loops — employees had no sense of how they were performing

⚙️ Interventions Designed

  • Information: Clear job descriptions and 90-day onboarding roadmap
  • Job Design: Aligned tasks to developer specialization; formed cross-functional pods
  • Empowerment: Monthly 1-on-1 feedback + individual development plans
  • Training: Bi-weekly internal knowledge sharing sessions

📈 Results After 12 Months

12%
Turnover dropped from 40%
2×
Faster time-to-productivity
4.6★
Glassdoor rating (was 3.1)
31%
Feature delivery speed increase
💡

Key Lesson: The real issue wasn't pay — it was lack of information, unclear job design, and no empowerment structures. EHP's systematic approach identified the true causes and led to targeted, cost-effective solutions.

Reference

Key Terms Glossary

Quick reference for essential Engineering Human Performance terminology.

Engineering Human Performance (EHP)
A systematic approach to analyze, diagnose, and improve human performance in the workplace through evidence-based interventions.
Performance Gap
The difference between the expected (desired) level of performance and the actual (current) level of performance.
Root Cause Analysis
A method for identifying the underlying cause(s) of a performance problem, rather than just addressing surface-level symptoms.
Performance Intervention
A deliberate action designed to close a performance gap — ranging from training programs to environmental or structural redesign.
HPT Model
Human Performance Technology Model (Van Tiem et al., 2012) — a framework covering Analysis, Intervention Selection, Implementation, and Evaluation.
Instructional Intervention
Solutions that address skill or knowledge deficits, such as training programs, e-learning modules, coaching, or mentoring.
Non-Instructional Intervention
Solutions that address environmental or organizational factors — process redesign, job aids, incentives, or structural changes.
Formative Evaluation
Ongoing evaluation conducted during the design and implementation process to make real-time improvements to the intervention.
